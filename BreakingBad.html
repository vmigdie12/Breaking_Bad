Introduction

Breaking Bad is an American television series created by Vince Gilligan, which aired between 2008 and 2013. The series premiered on January 20, 2008 and is a production of Sony Pictures Television. Breaking Bad has been enthusiastically acclaimed by many critics and the public and is considered one of the best television series of all time. In the United States and Canada it was broadcast on the AMC network, and in 2013 it was one of the most watched cable television programs there.

Breaking Bad follows protagonist Walter White, a chemistry teacher who lives in New Mexico with his wife and teenage son who has cerebral palsy. White is diagnosed with Stage III cancer and given a prognosis of two years left to live. With a new sense of fearlessness based on his medical prognosis, and a desire to secure his family's financial security, White chooses to enter a dangerous world of drugs and crime and ascends to power in the world. The series explores how a fatal diagnosis such as White's releases a typical man from the daily concerns and constraints of normal society and follows his transformation from mild family man to a kingpin of the drug trade.

Characters

Walter White (Bryan Cranston): Chemistry professor diagnosed with terminal cancer. He becomes the meth cook known as "Heisenberg" to secure his family's financial future.

Jesse Pinkman (Aaron Paul): Walter's former student and partner in methamphetamine manufacturing. Initially a drug dealer, Jesse struggles with his morality throughout the series.

Skyler White (Anna Gunn): Walter's wife, who is initially unaware of her husband's illegal activities. She eventually becomes involved in Walter's business and is affected by the consequences.

Hank Schrader (Dean Norris): Walter's brother-in-law and DEA agent. Throughout the series, Hank actively investigates drug trafficking, unaware of his brother-in-law's involvement in criminal activity.

Saul Goodman (Bob Odenkirk): A cunning criminal lawyer known for his unconventional methods. He becomes Walter and Jesse's lawyer and later stars in his own spin-off, "Better Call Saul".
